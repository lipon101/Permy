Legal & Data License

Last updated: July 10, 2026 · v0.1

Permy is a developer-first API that surfaces publicly available building-permit and construction data. This page summarizes the terms of use, the data-license obligations you inherit, and our privacy stance. It is a summary, not legal advice — the definitive terms live in the OpenAPI termsOfService field and any executed agreement.

1. Source code license

The Permy reference implementation is released under Apache-2.0. You may use, modify, and self-host it under the terms of that license. The hosted API service at permy.onrender.com and on RapidAPI is operated as a commercial service and is not governed by the open-source license.

2. Data sources & upstream licenses

Permy ingests only public records and official open-data APIs — Socrata, ArcGIS Hub, Accela, Tyler, and CKAN portals operated by U.S. cities and counties. We do not scrape gated or ToS-protected sources. Each permit record carries its own provenance:

  • source_url — a direct link to the originating city record.
  • source_name — the human-readable name of the portal.
  • last_checked_at — when we last verified the record upstream.

You inherit the license terms of each upstream jurisdiction. Most U.S. municipal open-data portals publish under permissive terms (often CC0, ODbL, or a custom open license), but obligations vary — attribution requirements, redistribution limits, and commercial-use terms differ by city. The /v1/coverage endpoint reports each jurisdiction's source_portal and source_name so you can identify and comply with the relevant license. When in doubt, consult the upstream portal's terms linked from source_url.

Redistribution. Because upstream terms vary, bulk redistribution of raw permit records may be restricted by some jurisdictions. The normalized, derived intelligence Permy adds (lead scores, development scores, confidence, recommended actions, narratives) is original work and is not subject to the upstream data license.

3. Acceptable use

  • Public records only. Permy exposes data that is already public. We do not surface gated, paywalled, or ToS-protected municipal data.
  • No PII resale. You may not resell homeowner personal information as a standalone product. Building-product lead generation, contractor analytics, and proptech use cases are permitted.
  • Rate-limit respect. Do not attempt to bypass the documented rate limits or the RapidAPI quota system.
  • Honesty in freshness. Do not present cached or archive data as live. Permy labels archive sources explicitly; preserve those labels in downstream UIs.

4. Privacy

Building permits are public records and may contain property-owner names and contractor contact details as published by the jurisdiction. Permy:

  • Minimizes homeowner personal data per each city's published license.
  • Does not sell personal data.
  • Does not log or retain your API query parameters beyond what is needed for billing and abuse prevention.
  • Is GDPR-aware — a Data Processing Addendum (DPA) is available on the Enterprise tier.

5. No warranty; "data as of"

Permitted data is provided "as is." Municipal portals can lag, change schema, or go temporarily offline. Every response includes freshness signals (last_checked_at, last_ingested_at) and confidence scores so you can make informed decisions. We clearly label archive sources when an upstream feed goes dark — we never serve stale data as fresh.
