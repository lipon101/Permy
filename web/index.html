What you actually get

  • ✓Decision-scored. A 0–100 lead_score + recommended_action (call_now / qualify / monitor / skip) so you don't just get data, you get a recommendation.
  • ⛓Source provenance on every record — source_url, source_name, last_checked_at. Always click through to the city's own page.
  • ○Explicit null, never omitted. Austin didn't publish a valuation here — it's null, not missing, so your parser never breaks.
  • ★Confidence score (0–1) blends source trust + freshness + field completeness — surface "verified" vs "unverified" in your UI.
  • !DQ flags (valuation_unknown, no_phone, weak_geocode) tell you exactly what's thin so your app can decide.
Concepts · Lead scoring

A deterministic score you can audit.

Same inputs always produce the same score — no LLM in the hot path. Six components, persona-adjustable weights, transparent bands.

lead_score = round( … ) · 0–100
recency × w_recency
+ valuation × w_valuation
+ trade_fit × w_trade_fit
+ property_fit × w_property_fit
+ contactability× w_contactability
+ momentum × w_momentum
Each component scored 0–1, scaled by its weight (summing to 100). Weights shift by persona — a roofer weights recency + trade fit; an investor weights value + multi-family + ZIP momentum; a supplier weights commercial volume + market heat.
